Periodic Review & Rebalancing: Keeping Your Shariah Portfolio on Track

Maintain disciplined growth and risk management with structured portfolio reviews and rebalancing strategies.

Introduction

Even the most carefully constructed Shariah-compliant portfolio requires ongoing attention. Periodic review and rebalancing are essential to ensure that investments remain aligned with family goals, risk tolerance, and market developments.

Why Review Matters

Financial markets evolve, and portfolios drift from their intended allocations over time. Regular reviews help identify underperforming funds, exposure imbalances, and opportunities for ethical growth without compromising Shariah compliance.

Rebalancing Strategies

Rebalancing restores the intended allocation by adjusting fund proportions. This disciplined approach reduces concentration risk, reinforces risk management, and maintains long-term growth objectives while respecting Islamic investment principles.

Frequency and Best Practices

Annual or semi-annual reviews are recommended. Monitoring economic trends, compliance updates, and personal circumstances ensures portfolios stay on track. Combining review with informed adjustments preserves both capital and Shariah integrity.

Conclusion
